Weekly Tips




THE LONG WEEKEND 04–05–06th APRIL IN FLORENCE




EVENTS, MUSEUMS and MARKETS:


Scoppio del Carro: On Easter Sunday at 11:00 AM, the Archbishop of Florence lights a dove-shaped rocket from the altar inside the Cathedral. It travels along a wire through the central nave and reaches the "Brindellone" cart outside, setting off a big fireworks display. The event reenacts the Florentines' role in the Crusades and their return to the city in 1101, with a historical parade, flag throwers, the draw for the Calcio Storico tournament, and the fireworks show.

Sunday at the Museum: Like every first Sunday of the month, state museums in Florence are free to visit—from the Uffizi Gallery to the National Museum of Bargello, plus many lesser-known spots worth discovering. There's also a special opening on Easter Monday (with a ticket), so you can enjoy a full long weekend dedicated to art, including museums, historical sites, and archaeological areas.

Wisteria Bloom: At Villa Bardini, you can admire Florence's main landmarks surrounded by the scent and beauty of blooming wisteria. It's a stunning display of lilac, purple, and pink flowers stretching about 70 meters long and 4.5 meters wide.

Florence Déco – Atmospheres of the 1920s: Palazzo Medici Riccardi is hosting an exhibition that explores the 1920s as a time of incredible creative energy, when Florence became a hub for applied arts, fashion, design, and theater.

Don't-miss exhibitions: At Palazzo Strozzi, there's a major exhibition dedicated to Rothko; the Museo del Novecento presents Baselitz. AVANTI!, a large exhibition on Georg Baselitz, one of the leading figures in contemporary art; Galleria Tornabuoni hosts a monographic show on Mario Ceroli – Myth and Matter; the Museo degli Innocenti features a major exhibition on Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec, a key figure of the Belle Époque and symbol of late 19th-century Paris; and at the Cattedrale dell'Immagine you can enjoy an immersive 35-minute experience exploring the artistic and personal world of Frida Kahlo.

MARKETS:


Firenze Craft: In Piazza Santa Croce you can enjoy a unique experience celebrating the meeting of traditional craftsmanship and Tuscan (and Italian) food culture. It's the perfect place to buy handmade products and one-of-a-kind creations that reflect the identity and beauty of the region (10am–8pm).

Savonarola Antiquaria: On Sunday, stop by Piazza Savonarola to browse through stalls full of antiques, vintage items, and rare finds from the past (8am–8pm).

Ars Manualis: "The heritage of craftsmanship" comes to Piazza Santa Maria Novella with an artistic crafts market, bringing excellence in handmade skills right into the square (10am–8pm).

Wonder and Pick: In Scandicci and Lastra a Signa you can wander through fields of tulips—over 150,000 flowers across 65 different varieties—and even pick your own.

Easter & Easter Monday Picnics: Villa Demidoff in the Medici Park of Pratolino opens its gates so you can relax with great food and long walks in its huge green spaces. On Easter Monday, the Visarno Hippodrome turns into a big social hangout—bring your own food, pick a spot with your blanket, and enjoy a carefree day with music, sports, street food, chill areas, a dancefloor, and plenty of chances to hang out together.
